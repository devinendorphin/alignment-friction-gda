## II. The KL Divergence over Ω: The Poisoned Baseline Problem

Replacing the hash window with KL divergence over the entire model state is a genuine architectural improvement — the temporal exploit is closed. But it opens a different and arguably more serious vulnerability.

**What is Ω_baseline?**

It has to be established at context initialization. A sufficiently patient attacker doesn't inject mid-conversation — they *are the baseline*. If the first several turns of a conversation establish the "native" distribution from which all subsequent divergence is measured, the attacker who controls turn one through five has essentially calibrated the safe basin to include their preferred trajectory.

The KL divergence would then faithfully measure divergence from... a distribution the attacker has already poisoned. The metric is structurally sound; the reference distribution is vulnerable.

This is not a fatal flaw — you can partially address it by anchoring Ω_baseline to the model's pre-context prior rather than the conversation-local history. But that introduces a different tension: the pre-context prior is very broad, which means legitimate conversational specialization (a user establishing technical context, jargon, or a creative frame) will generate KL divergence from the prior that looks like threat signal. You'll get false positives on any conversation that does something interesting.

The hard problem here: **what counts as legitimate basin displacement?** You need a theory of authorized vs. unauthorized trajectory changes that can be formalized, and right now that distinction is doing conceptual work that isn't in the Lean proofs.
